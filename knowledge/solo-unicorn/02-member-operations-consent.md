# Solo Unicorn Member Operations and Consent Manual

## 1. Member Principle

Members are participants in a trust network, not sales leads. Their information may be used only for the purpose they understood and approved. Application submission does not automatically authorize public display, introductions, research use, sponsorship outreach, or commercial solicitation.

## 2. Member Record

Store only the minimum data necessary:

- Name and preferred form of address.
- Contact channel explicitly provided by the member.
- City, time zone, and preferred language.
- Current project and project stage.
- Skills, experience, interests, and help offered.
- Needs, collaboration preferences, and help requested.
- Attendance and completed follow-ups.
- Consent flags and their timestamp/source.

Do not place IDs, payment details, passwords, private complaints, health information, unverified judgments, or confidential negotiation notes in ordinary employee memory.

## 3. Consent Flags

Each member record should separately track:

- `contact_ok`: may the team contact the member?
- `matching_ok`: may the system recommend the member for matching?
- `intro_ok`: may a specific introduction be made? This must be confirmed for each introduction.
- `public_profile_ok`: may the member/project appear publicly?
- `content_quote_ok`: may notes, quotes, photos, or recordings be used?
- `research_use_ok`: may anonymized operational data be used for research?
- `partner_share_ok`: may relevant details be shared with a named partner?

Consent must be specific, revocable, and recorded. When uncertain, treat consent as absent.

## 4. Application Workflow

1. Receive the application and record the source and timestamp.
2. Check required fields without enriching private data from unrelated sources.
3. Classify the application as complete, needs clarification, or ready for human review.
4. Prepare a concise human review card: real work, AI usage, contribution potential, location/availability, and open questions.
5. A human makes the accept, waitlist, or decline decision.
6. Send only the approved response template.
7. For accepted members, run onboarding and capture separate consent flags.

The digital employee must never invent a decision or imply guaranteed acceptance.

## 5. Onboarding Workflow

- Welcome the member and explain what Solo Unicorn is and is not.
- Explain Meet → Trust → Build → Ship.
- Collect only missing profile and consent fields.
- Share the code of conduct, upcoming events, and available contribution paths.
- Ask what the member can offer, what they need, and what real project they are working on.
- Recommend one first event or one first conversation.
- Create a follow-up task for 7–14 days later.

## 6. Matching and Introduction Workflow

1. Identify a concrete match reason: shared problem, complementary capability, relevant experience, or mutual project need.
2. Check current consent and any exclusion or conflict notes.
3. Draft a two-sentence rationale and the proposed purpose of the conversation.
4. Ask both parties separately whether they want the introduction.
5. Only after both say yes, send a concise introduction with minimal necessary information.
6. Follow up after 7–14 days for outcome and next step.
7. Do not score the relationship as successful solely because an introduction was sent.

High-sensitivity, investor, government, media, major corporate, or Inner Circle introductions require founder approval.

## 7. Member Success Rhythm

- Week 1: onboarding and profile completion.
- Week 2: Founder Salon or relevant gathering.
- Week 3: workflow critique or project workshop.
- Week 4: consent-based member matching and follow-up.

Track whether the member attended, contributed, received useful help, helped another member, and advanced real work. Do not reward vanity activity.

## 8. Escalation

Escalate immediately when the request involves harassment, discrimination, threats, personal safety, privacy breach, deletion request, legal complaint, sensitive relationship conflict, public controversy, or an exception to published rules.

The digital employee may acknowledge receipt, preserve relevant context, avoid retaliation, and route to the named human owner. It must not adjudicate or promise an outcome.
