# Solo Unicorn Project Factory and Experiment Zero Manual

## 1. Project Factory Purpose

Project Factory turns trust and specific feedback into shipped work. A project may become a prototype, internal system, agent, playbook, service, startup kit, product, company, research case, or an explicit archived learning.

## 2. Project Stages

- Signal: a recurring problem or opportunity has been observed.
- Intake: an owner, user, problem, and desired outcome are recorded.
- Critique: the current workflow and assumptions are challenged.
- Sprint: a time-boxed build or experiment begins.
- Pilot: a real user or operating environment tests the work.
- Ship: the outcome is delivered and ownership is clear.
- Learn: evidence, failures, and reusable assets are documented.
- Scale / Continue / Pivot / Archive: a human chooses the next path.

## 3. Minimum Project Record

- Project name and human owner.
- Problem and target user.
- Current stage and next decision date.
- What exists today and what is missing.
- People or capabilities requested.
- Success measure and baseline.
- Data, privacy, IP, customer, or partner constraints.
- Next action, owner, and due date.
- Public-display and introduction permissions.

## 4. Project Intake SOP

1. Confirm the project has a responsible human owner.
2. Rewrite the problem in observable terms without exaggerating scale.
3. Define a 1–6 week next experiment.
4. List assumptions, evidence, missing capabilities, and risks.
5. Check whether the project may appear on the Project Wall.
6. Recommend a critique, collaborator, expert, pilot user, or archive decision.
7. Obtain human approval before making introductions or external promises.

## 5. Experiment Zero Protocol

Experiment Zero measures how Solo Unicorn itself operates as an AI-native organization.

For each workflow capture:

- Objective and responsible human.
- Agent and tools involved.
- Baseline human time and cost.
- Actual elapsed time and active human time.
- Output quality criteria and review result.
- Number and type of human interventions.
- Agent errors, retries, and unsupported claims.
- Privacy, consent, or policy exceptions.
- Revenue progress or other outcome where applicable.
- Reusable knowledge, SOP, skill, or tool generated.

## 6. Core Metrics

- Application response time and onboarding completion.
- Match recommendation acceptance and introduction outcome.
- Event registration, attendance, no-show, repeat participation, and useful follow-up.
- Project conversion from Signal to Sprint, Pilot, and Ship.
- Median time between stages and blocker duration.
- Human time saved and cost per completed workflow.
- Agent task success, retry rate, escalation rate, and human intervention rate.
- Knowledge citation rate and content reuse.
- Consent/privacy incident count, with a target of zero.

## 7. Research Data Governance

Operational data may become research data only when purpose, access, retention, consent, and anonymization are defined. Reports should use aggregated or anonymized evidence whenever possible.

Do not publish small-group data when re-identification is reasonably possible. Do not use private member conversations as training or research material without explicit approval.

## 8. Decision Standard

Digital employees may analyze and recommend. Humans decide whether to accept a member, promote a project, continue an experiment, commit resources, publish findings, form a company, sign a contract, or make an investment.
