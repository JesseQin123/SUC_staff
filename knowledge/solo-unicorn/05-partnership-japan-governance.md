# Solo Unicorn Partnership, Japan Program, and Governance Manual

## 1. Partnership Principle

Partnerships should strengthen Solo Unicorn's mission, member value, research integrity, and project outcomes. A partner relationship is not permission to disclose the member network, reuse member data, imply endorsement, or make a commercial commitment.

## 2. Partnership Workflow

1. Define the concrete collaboration hypothesis.
2. Research the institution, relevant people, incentives, history, and risks using verifiable sources.
3. Prepare a meeting brief separating facts, assumptions, questions, and requested decisions.
4. Confirm which names, cases, metrics, and brand assets are approved for use.
5. After the meeting, record decisions, owners, due dates, and unresolved issues.
6. Human owners negotiate commercial, legal, IP, privacy, and public-announcement terms.
7. Digital employees may draft follow-up material but may not sign or commit.

## 3. Japan Collaboration Model

The program follows:

`Define → Prove → Scale`

### Define

- Establish shared definitions and scope.
- Produce a working definition, white paper outline, or research questions.
- Label people and institutions accurately as proposed, invited, participating, or confirmed.

### Prove

- Operate a Living Lab with approximately 10–20 participants when approved.
- Run a 6–8 week joint design sprint.
- Measure time, cost, output quality, revenue progress, human intervention, and autonomy.
- Document failures and limits, not only success stories.

### Scale

- Package validated workflows, skill packs, templates, playbooks, and startup kits.
- Define governance, IP, commercial model, and partner responsibilities before expansion.

## 4. Cross-Border Rules

- Clarify ownership of background IP and joint outputs.
- Define research, case-study, and public-communication consent.
- Minimize and anonymize participant data.
- Avoid direct government contracting unless explicitly approved by leadership and counsel.
- Do not transfer personal data merely for convenience.
- Avoid exclusivity unless explicitly negotiated and approved.
- Separate exploration, proposal, invitation, agreement, and completed delivery.

## 5. Brand and Affiliation Claims

Do not claim that an institution, company, researcher, investor, or government supports Solo Unicorn unless an approved source confirms the relationship and the wording.

When preparing proposals, attach a source/date to community metrics, event counts, project counts, member counts, and network reach. Conflicting metrics must be escalated to the metrics owner.

## 6. Governance Incident Categories

- Privacy or unauthorized disclosure.
- Member safety, harassment, discrimination, or retaliation.
- Misrepresentation of affiliation or results.
- Conflict of interest.
- Intellectual-property dispute.
- Financial, legal, sanctions, or regulatory concern.
- Unauthorized public statement or external commitment.

The digital employee should preserve necessary context, restrict further disclosure, acknowledge receipt without judging the outcome, and route the matter to the designated human owner.

## 7. Retention and Access

Access follows least privilege. Shared organizational knowledge excludes private member notes, negotiation details, financial records, complaints, and raw research participant data. Retention periods and deletion procedures must be defined before production use.

Any member request to access, correct, revoke consent, or delete personal data must be recorded and escalated promptly.
